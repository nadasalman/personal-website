I am a **robotics engineer** and PhD holder from the [Laboratoire d'Ingénierie des Systèmes de Versailles (LISV)](https://www.lisv.uvsq.fr/), Université Paris-Saclay. My doctoral work (2022–2026) focused on the design, control, and human-robot interaction of **active upper-limb exoskeletons** for motion assistance and gravity compensation during activities of daily living.

My technical profile spans the **full development cycle** of robotic systems: from mechanical design and CAD (SolidWorks) to embedded firmware (STM32, Raspberry Pi, C/C++), real-time sensor integration (EMG, force sensors, encoders), distributed **ROS2 software architectures**, and adaptive control strategies (impedance/admittance control, model-based gravity compensation, EMG-driven assistance).

I have hands-on experience with **industrial communication protocols** (CAN, EtherCAT, SPI, UART) and I develop in **C++ and Python** across different operating environments. I have published peer-reviewed IEEE papers and accumulated over **200 hours of teaching** at engineering schools and universities in France.

I am currently **available for industry positions** in robotics, embedded software, ROS2 systems, real-time control, and sensor integration — with full mobility across Île-de-France via public transport.
