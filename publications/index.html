Publications

IEEE peer-reviewed publications by Dr. Nada SALMAN — in reverse chronological order.

2026

  1. ICARA
    Real-Time Neuro-Motor Index Modulation for Adaptive Admittance Control in Upper-Limb Exoskeletons
    Nada SALMAN, Jinane Charafeddine, Swaminath Venkateswaran, and 1 more author
    In 2026 7th International Conference on Automation, Robotics and Applications (ICARA), 2026
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    This paper presents a real-time method for neuro-motor index modulation to enable adaptive admittance control in upper-limb exoskeletons, using surface EMG signals to continuously adapt assistance levels during motor tasks.

  2. ICAISET
    Beyond Benchmark Accuracy: Toward Clinically Trustworthy AI for Biomedical Signals
    Jinane Charafeddine, T. Houda, Nada SALMAN, and 1 more author
    In 2026 International Conference on Artificial Intelligence, Systems Engineering and Technology (ICAISET), 2026
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    This work argues that benchmark accuracy alone is insufficient for clinical AI systems processing biomedical signals, and proposes evaluation criteria that better reflect trustworthiness in real-world rehabilitation contexts.

  3. CSNT
    HITL-Adaptive AI for Safe and Personalized Upper-Limb Rehabilitation
    A. M. Alotaibi, Nada SALMAN, T. Houda, and 3 more authors
    In 2026 International Conference on Communication Systems and Network Technologies (CSNT), 2026
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    We propose a human-in-the-loop (HITL) framework for adaptive AI in upper-limb rehabilitation, enabling personalised and safe assistance by continuously incorporating user feedback into the control policy.

2024

  1. RO-MAN
    Adaptive Weight Compensation in Assistive Upper-Limb Exoskeletons: an EMG Analysis
    Nada SALMAN and Abderraouf Benali
    In 2024 33rd IEEE International Conference on Robot and Human Interactive Communication (RO-MAN), 2024
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    We analyse surface EMG signals to drive adaptive weight compensation in an assistive upper-limb exoskeleton, demonstrating automatic assistance adaptation during flexion-extension movements with load.
